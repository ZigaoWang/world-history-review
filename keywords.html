World History Keywords

Comprehensive Review Guide

By Zigao Wang

Middle Ages

Fall of Rome The end of Roman rule in Western Europe
Middle Ages Period from about 500–1500 CE, also called the Medieval period
Dark Ages Early part of the Middle Ages, marked by decline
Germanic Kingdoms Tribes like the Franks, Visigoths, and Saxons
Feudalism System where land is exchanged for service and loyalty
Manor The lord's estate; center of medieval life
Serf Peasant tied to the land
Vassal Person who received land in exchange for service

Islamic Empire

Muhammad Founder of Islam
Islam Religion based on the teachings of Muhammad
Caliph Successor to Muhammad; leader of the Muslim community
Five Pillars Five basic acts of worship in Islam
Golden Age of Islam Period of great cultural and scientific achievement

Renaissance and Reformation

Renaissance Period of renewed interest in art and learning
Humanism Focus on human potential and achievements
Secularism Focus on worldly, not religious, matters
Printing Press Machine for printing text or pictures
Protestant Reformation Movement to reform the Catholic Church

Exploration and Colonization

Columbian Exchange Transfer of plants, animals, and diseases between Old and New Worlds
Triangular Trade Trade between Europe, Africa, and the Americas
Enlightenment Movement for reason and rights
Social Contract Agreement between people and government

Industrial Revolution and Modern Era

Industrialization Growth of factories and machine production
Capitalism Economic system with private ownership
Socialism Economic system with public ownership
Imperialism Domination of one country by another
MANIA Causes of WWI (Militarism, Alliances, Nationalism, Imperialism, Assassination)
