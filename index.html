World History Timeline

Grade 8 Comprehensive Review

By Zigao Wang

Unit 1: European Middle Ages & Early Islamic Empire

476 CE

Fall of Western Roman Empire

What happened: The Western Roman Empire collapsed due to invasions, weak leadership, and economic troubles.

Key Points:

  • Led to chaos in Europe
  • Loss of central government
  • Start of the Middle Ages

Keywords:

Fall of Rome The end of Roman rule in Western Europe
Middle Ages Period from about 500–1500 CE, also called the Medieval period
500-800 CE

The Dark Ages

What happened: Europe became fragmented; learning and trade declined.

Key Points:

  • Rise of small kingdoms
  • Little progress in science or culture
  • Germanic tribes established new kingdoms

Keywords:

Dark Ages Early part of the Middle Ages, marked by decline
Germanic Kingdoms Tribes like the Franks, Visigoths, and Saxons
768-814 CE

Charlemagne's Reign

What happened: Charlemagne united much of Western Europe and was crowned Holy Roman Emperor.

Key Points:

  • Promoted education
  • Spread Christianity
  • Created a strong government

Keywords:

Charlemagne King of the Franks, first Holy Roman Emperor
Holy Roman Empire Empire in central Europe, started by Charlemagne
800-1000 CE

Rise of Feudalism

What happened: Kings granted land to nobles in exchange for loyalty and military service.

Key Points:

  • Society organized in strict hierarchy
  • Peasants worked the land
  • System based on mutual obligations

Keywords:

Feudalism System where land is exchanged for service and loyalty
Manor The lord's estate; center of medieval life
Serf Peasant tied to the land
Vassal Person who received land in exchange for service
610-750 CE

Rise and Spread of Islam

What happened: Muhammad founded Islam in Arabia; the religion spread rapidly.

Key Points:

  • United the Arabian Peninsula
  • Created a vast empire
  • Established new religious and political system

Keywords:

Muhammad Founder of Islam
Islam Religion based on the teachings of Muhammad
Caliph Successor to Muhammad; leader of the Muslim community
Five Pillars Five basic acts of worship in Islam
750-1258 CE

Islamic Golden Age

What happened: Muslim scholars made advances in science, math, medicine, and art.

Key Points:

  • Baghdad became a center of learning
  • Preserved Greek and Roman knowledge
  • Major advances in mathematics and science

Keywords:

Golden Age of Islam Period of great cultural and scientific achievement
House of Wisdom Major center of learning in Baghdad
1096-1291 CE

The Crusades

What happened: European Christians launched military campaigns to capture the Holy Land from Muslims.

Key Points:

  • Increased trade between Europe and Middle East
  • Cultural exchange between civilizations
  • Heightened religious tensions

Keywords:

Crusades Religious wars between Christians and Muslims
Holy Land Area around Jerusalem, sacred to Jews, Christians, and Muslims
Pope Urban II Pope who called for the First Crusade

Unit 2: Mongols, Renaissance, and Reformation

1206-1368 CE

Mongol Empire

What happened: Genghis Khan united Mongol tribes and conquered a vast empire across Asia and Europe.

Key Points:

  • Created safe trade routes (Silk Road)
  • Spread ideas and technology
  • Largest contiguous land empire in history

Keywords:

Genghis Khan Founder of the Mongol Empire
Silk Road Trade routes connecting China and Europe
1300-1600 CE

The Renaissance

What happened: "Rebirth" of art, science, and learning in Europe, starting in Italy.

Key Points:

  • Focus on humanism and classical learning
  • Individual achievement celebrated
  • Major advances in art and science

Keywords:

Renaissance Period of renewed interest in art and learning
Humanism Focus on human potential and achievements
Secularism Focus on worldly, not religious, matters
Patron Wealthy supporter of artists
Perspective Artistic technique for showing depth
Famous Artists

Renaissance Masters

Notable Figures:

  • Leonardo da Vinci: Artist, inventor, scientist (Mona Lisa, The Last Supper)
  • Michelangelo: Sculptor, painter (David, Sistine Chapel)
  • Machiavelli: Political thinker (The Prince)
1450 CE

Invention of the Printing Press

What happened: Johannes Gutenberg invented the movable-type printing press.

Key Points:

  • Books became cheaper and more available
  • Ideas spread quickly
  • Increased literacy rates

Keywords:

Printing Press Machine for printing text or pictures
Gutenberg Inventor of the printing press
Vernacular Everyday language
1517 CE

Protestant Reformation

What happened: Martin Luther protested Church corruption (selling indulgences) and started a split in Christianity.

Key Points:

  • Led to new Christian churches (Protestantism)
  • Religious wars and conflicts
  • Reforms in the Catholic Church

Keywords:

Martin Luther German monk who started the Reformation
95 Theses List of complaints against the Catholic Church
Indulgence Church pardon for sins
Protestant Christian who broke away from the Catholic Church
Reformation Movement to reform the Catholic Church

Unit 3: Exploration, Colonization, and Enlightenment

1400-1600 CE

Age of Exploration

What happened: European countries explored the world by sea, seeking new trade routes and wealth.

Key Points:

  • Led to discovery of the Americas
  • Established global trade networks
  • Started the era of colonization

Keywords:

Columbus Italian explorer who reached the Americas in 1492
Conquistador Spanish conqueror
Caravel Fast, small sailing ship
1492 CE

Columbus Reaches the Americas

What happened: Columbus landed in the Caribbean, beginning European colonization.

Key Points:

  • Led to the Columbian Exchange
  • Conquest of native empires
  • Beginning of European colonization

Keywords:

Columbian Exchange Transfer of plants, animals, and diseases between Old and New Worlds
1500-1800 CE

Colonization and the Atlantic Slave Trade

What happened: Spain, Portugal, England, and France established colonies in the Americas, Africa, and Asia.

Key Points:

  • Native populations declined
  • Millions of Africans enslaved
  • European empires grew wealthy

Keywords:

Encomienda Spanish system of forced labor
Triangular Trade Trade between Europe, Africa, and the Americas
Chattel Slavery People treated as property
1600-1800 CE

The Enlightenment

What happened: Philosophers promoted reason, science, and individual rights.

Key Points:

  • Inspired revolutions
  • New ideas about government
  • Scientific advancement

Keywords:

Enlightenment Movement for reason and rights
John Locke Philosopher who argued for natural rights
Social Contract Agreement between people and government
Voltaire Philosopher for freedom of speech and religion
1776 CE

American Revolution

What happened: American colonies declared independence from Britain.

Key Points:

  • Inspired by Enlightenment ideas
  • Led to creation of the United States
  • Model for other revolutions

Keywords:

Revolution Overthrow of a government
1789 CE

French Revolution

What happened: French people overthrew their king and established a republic.

Key Points:

  • Spread ideas of liberty and equality
  • End of absolute monarchy in France
  • Rise of nationalism

Keywords:

Nationalism Pride in one's nation

Unit 4: Industrial Revolution, Imperialism, and World War I

1750-1850 CE

Industrial Revolution

What happened: Major change from hand-made to machine-made goods; factories and cities grew.

Key Points:

  • New inventions (steam engine, spinning jenny)
  • Growth of cities (urbanization)
  • Rise of capitalism and socialism

Keywords:

Industrialization Growth of factories and machine production
Urbanization Growth of cities
Steam Engine Machine using steam power
Capitalism Economic system with private ownership
Socialism Economic system with public ownership
1800-1914 CE

Age of Imperialism

What happened: European powers colonized much of Africa and Asia.

Key Points:

  • Motivated by economic gain
  • Competition between European powers
  • Justified by racism ("White Man's Burden")

Keywords:

Imperialism Domination of one country by another
Colony Land controlled by a foreign nation
"White Man's Burden" Belief that Europeans had a duty to "civilize" others
1914-1918 CE

World War I

What happened: Global war mainly in Europe, caused by MANIA (Militarism, Alliances, Nationalism, Imperialism, Assassination).

Key Points:

  • New weapons and trench warfare
  • Millions killed
  • Map of Europe changed

Keywords:

MANIA Causes of WWI (Militarism, Alliances, Nationalism, Imperialism, Assassination)
Trench Warfare Fighting from ditches
Alliance Partnership between countries
Armistice Agreement to stop fighting
